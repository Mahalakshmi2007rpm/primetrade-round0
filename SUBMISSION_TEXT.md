Summary of key findings:
- Fear days show higher average daily PnL but higher volatility than Greed days.
- Directional long bias is stronger during Fear, while Greed shifts toward short-heavy directional mix.
- Frequent and consistent traders are more robust performers across sentiment regimes.
- Larger size cohorts can generate higher absolute PnL, but with weaker win rates.

Actionable rules:
- In Fear regimes, favor frequent directional traders and cap aggressive size expansion unless win rate remains strong.
- In Greed regimes, tighten position sizing and prioritize consistent accounts.
